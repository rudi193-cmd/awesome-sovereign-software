# The Sovereignty Report 2026

> **DRAFT v0.2** — receipts verified against sources 2026-07-03 (linked inline). Corrections
> welcome via [issues](https://github.com/rudi193-cmd/awesome-sovereign-software/issues).

The [Sovereignty Test](../README.md#the-sovereignty-test) asks five questions of any piece of
software: does it run without an **account**, without a **server**, without a **subscription**;
is your **data readable** without it; and does it **survive the vendor**?

[Awesome Sovereign Software](../README.md) catalogs the apps that pass. This report runs the
same five questions against the software most people actually live inside — and scores it.

## Method, and a fairness note

Each product is scored on the five criteria as most people use it: ✅ pass (1 point),
⚠️ partial (½), ❌ fail (0). Maximum 5.

To be fair: most of these are *services by design*. Nobody at Spotify is hiding the fact that
it's a subscription to licensed music. The point of scoring them isn't gotcha — it's that the
sum of these designs is a life where you own remarkably little of what you rely on, and most
people have never seen that added up in one table. Where a product does something right
(genuine export, open formats), it's credited.

One recurring distinction matters: **your data vs. licensed content.** Your photos, notes,
health records, and messages are yours; the movies and music in a streaming library never were.
The test is hardest on products that treat the first category like the second.

## The receipts

Scores are abstract. What "fails the test" means in practice:

- **Amazon [remotely deleted purchased copies of *Nineteen Eighty-Four* and *Animal Farm* from
  Kindles](https://www.theregister.com/2009/07/18/amazon_removes_1984_from_kindle/)** (July 2009,
  with refunds) — the canonical demonstration that a DRM library is access, not ownership. In
  February 2025 Amazon then
  [removed the "Download & Transfer via USB" option](https://goodereader.com/blog/kindle/amazon-removing-download-and-transfer-on-the-kindle-feb-26th),
  closing the main path people used to back up purchased books.
- **Evernote [capped its free tier at 50 notes and 1 notebook](https://techcrunch.com/2023/11/29/its-official-evernote-will-restrict-free-users-to-50-notes/)**
  (December 2023, a year after the Bending Spoons acquisition) — existing notes stayed viewable
  and exportable, but creating anything new became hostage to an upgrade prompt.
- **LastPass [was breached in 2022](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2022_LastPass_data_breach)**
  and attackers exfiltrated backups of customer vaults — encrypted fields plus plaintext
  metadata like website URLs — the failure mode of "someone else's server" arriving all at once.
  By 2025, US investigators had
  [linked a $150M cryptocurrency heist](https://krebsonsecurity.com/2025/03/feds-link-150m-cyberheist-to-2022-lastpass-hacks/)
  to vaults cracked from that breach. (Credit where due: LastPass CSV export works, which is why
  it scores a ✅ on readable data — and why leaving was easy.)
- **Adobe's apps deactivate when the subscription lapses** — years of skill in a tool you must
  keep renting. In June 2024 the
  [US FTC and DOJ sued Adobe](https://www.ftc.gov/news-events/news/press-releases/2024/06/ftc-takes-action-against-adobe-executives-hiding-fees-preventing-consumers-easily-cancelling)
  over cancellation friction and hidden early-termination fees.
- **Spotify [bricked Car Thing](https://techcrunch.com/2024/05/30/spotify-begins-offering-car-thing-refunds-as-it-faces-lawsuit-over-bricking-the-streaming-device/)**
  — a device customers paid for, remotely deactivated December 2024; refunds began only after
  public backlash and a class-action filing.
- **Fitbit accounts are being retired into Google accounts** — new users have needed a Google
  account since 2023, and existing accounts
  [must migrate by May 2026](https://9to5google.com/2025/03/28/fitbit-google-account-2026/),
  after which unmigrated data is deleted. A decade of health data changes owners because one
  company bought another.
- **[Google's graveyard](https://killedbygoogle.com/)** — Reader (2013), Play Music (2020),
  Stadia (2023, with refunds) — is the base rate for "survives the vendor" on any cloud product,
  from the company with the most resources to keep things alive.
- **Ring moved previously free features behind its subscription** —
  [March 2024 brought a 25% price hike on the Basic plan](https://www.forbes.com/sites/barrycollins/2024/02/09/ring-customers-revolt-after-huge-price-hike/)
  alongside formerly free Ring Alarm features
  [becoming subscription-only](https://www.digitaltrends.com/home/ring-protect-price-hike-march-11-details/)
  — hardware on your own wall that records to someone else's cloud, rented back to you.

## Product notes

**Microsoft 365 — 3/5, the honest surprise.** The apps run locally and work offline; documents
are local files in ISO-standardized formats (`.docx` and `.xlsx` are ECMA/ISO specs) that dozens of
other suites read. What fails is the rental: lapse and the apps drop into
[reduced-functionality (read-only) mode](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/microsoft-365/commerce/subscriptions/what-if-my-subscription-expires).
Your data survives fine; your tools don't. *Exit: your files already work in [LibreOffice](../README.md#documents--office) — install it and stop paying.*

**Kindle — 1.5/5.** Pay-once purchases and offline reading are real. But the books are DRM-locked
to your Amazon account, the USB backup path was removed in 2025, and Amazon has demonstrated it
can reach into the device. *Exit: buy DRM-free where possible; manage the library with
[Calibre](../README.md#reading--media) and read on [KOReader](../README.md#reading--media).*

**WhatsApp — 1.5/5.** End-to-end encryption is genuinely good and there's no subscription. But
identity is a phone number on Meta's servers, export is a clunky per-chat affair, and the
network effect is the lock-in. *Exit: per-chat export for the history you care about; for a
no-server future, [Briar or Jami](../README.md#messaging) — with the honest caveat that your
contacts are the hard part.*

**Notion — 1.5/5.** Markdown/CSV export exists and
[offline mode finally arrived in August 2025](https://www.notion.com/releases/2025-08-19)
(desktop and mobile apps only), but the account is mandatory, databases flatten on export, and
the workspace lives on their infrastructure. *Exit: export while relations still mean something; rebuild in
[Obsidian or Logseq](../README.md#notes--knowledge), where the vault is a folder.*

**LastPass — 1.5/5.** See the receipt above. *Exit: CSV export into
[KeePassXC](../README.md#passwords--secrets); the vault becomes a file you control.*

**Google Photos — 1/5.** Takeout works, which is more than most; but metadata arrives as
JSON sidecars divorced from the images, and the free tier is a storage meter with your memories
on it. *Exit: Takeout now, then [digiKam](../README.md#photos) over a folder you own, synced
with [Syncthing](../README.md#files-sync--backup).*

**Adobe Creative Cloud — 1/5.** PSD files are readable by other tools (partial credit), but the
subscription is the product, offline use is
[a grace period of 30 days on monthly plans and 99 on annual](https://helpx.adobe.com/creative-cloud/kb/internet-connection-creative-cloud-apps.html),
and your muscle memory is the moat. *Exit: [GIMP, Krita, Inkscape](../README.md#creative-tools) — free in both senses,
with documented formats.*

**ChatGPT — 1/5.** Account required, everything server-side, free tier as a metered sample,
data export exists but the model — the thing you're actually using — is never yours.
*Exit: open-weight models via [Ollama or Jan](../README.md#local-ai) run on your own hardware
and can't be repriced, lobotomized, or discontinued under you. (For where the "sovereign AI"
framing does and doesn't hold, see
[The Illusion of Sovereign AI](https://rudi193.substack.com/p/the-illusion-of-sovereign-ai).)*

**Fitbit — 1/5.** Your resting heart rate for the last decade is in a cloud whose account
system is being replaced under you — migrate to a Google account by the deadline or the data
is deleted. Takeout export is partial credit. *Exit:
[Gadgetbridge](../README.md#phone--wearables) runs many of the same devices with no vendor
account at all — the data lands in a local database.*

**Evernote — 1/5.** ENEX export is real (partial credit) but it's an Evernote-shaped format,
and the 2023 free-tier cliff showed how fast terms can change under a decade of accumulated
notes. *Exit: ENEX converts to Markdown with open tools; land in
[Joplin](../README.md#notes--knowledge), which imports it natively.*

**Ring — 0.5/5.** Your own doorbell, on your own house, recording to a cloud you rent access
to, with features migrating behind the subscription over time. Clip download exists while you
pay. *Exit: this category's honest answer is different hardware — look for cameras with local
storage (RTSP/ONVIF) rather than an export button that expires with your subscription.*

**Spotify — 0.5/5.** The music was never yours and doesn't pretend to be — the licensed-content
caveat applies in full. What is yours (playlists, listening history — years of curation) has no
first-class export; third-party tools partially fill the gap. *Exit: rebuild the library you
actually own with local files and [mpv/VLC](../README.md#media-tools--archiving); playlist
migration tools can carry the curation out.*

## The pattern

Read down the table and the five criteria collapse into one: **the account is the server is
the subscription.** Once identity lives on the vendor's infrastructure, everything else follows
— the data gravitates to their storage, the pricing becomes rent, and vendor death becomes your
problem. The products that score above 1 are precisely those where some piece — the file
format, the export, the offline mode — was left outside the loop.

That's also why the fix isn't "export more often." Export is a fire escape; sovereignty is not
living in a burning building. Every app on [the list](../README.md) starts from files you hold,
which is why every entry there can state an exit plan in one sentence — and most of the products
above can't.

## What to do with this

Pick one row that stings and replace it this weekend — the
[Sovereign Stack](../README.md#the-sovereign-stack) is the shortcut. You don't have to leave
everything; you have to know what leaving costs *before* the vendor makes the decision for you.
